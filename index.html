Entire Friend Group Converges On Age 25 In What Experts Are Calling “Unprecedented”

Experts confirm the phenomenon "entirely expected"; members describe the condition as "reflective yet chaotic"
By Our Special Correspondent The 25 Club Gazette, Private Circulation
📰
Members of the 25 Club, photographed at an undisclosed location, sometime before all this. Gazette Photo Desk

In what experts are calling “an unprecedented convergence of quarter-century milestones,” an entire friend group is reportedly turning 25 within the span of a single calendar year. “It’s kinda crazy,” said one witness, who wished to remain anonymous. “Like, almost a decade since Sarita’s last class lmao.”

In response to this convergence, the group has organised what they are calling The 25 Club — a year-long initiative wherein each member, upon the occasion of their twenty-fifth birthday, is summoned to document their reflections, learnings, and general state of being an unc. The archive opens the moment the candles are blown out. Submissions, this correspondent understands, are non-negotiable.

“Be as raw and honest as you’d be if we’d all be in the same room together.”

— Founding Charter, The 25 Club, Clause I, Subsection: Not LinkedIn

Sources close to the operation confirm, emphatically, that this is “not LinkedIn.” Participants have been explicitly instructed to dispense with the inspirational bullet points and professional-sounding epiphanies, and instead commit to the kind of honesty only achievable in a group chat at 1am.

Early reports suggest topics will include: what you’d tell your 18-year-old self, what mattered way less than you thought, and how it feels to be a quarter of a century old. By year’s end, the group expects to have compiled a complete record of honest takes on 25 years of being alive. When asked for comment, participants simply replied: “hopefully soon :’)”
