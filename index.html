Local Cohort Unanimously Reaches Age of 25 This Calendar Year

Experts confirm the phenomenon "entirely expected"; members describe the condition as "reflective yet chaotic"
By Our Special Correspondent The 25 Club Gazette, Private Circulation
📰
Members of the 25 Club, photographed at an undisclosed location, 2025. Gazette Photo Desk

A London, 2025 — Sources close to the group describe the mood as "reflective yet chaotic." The club remains strictly private, its rolls accessible only to those carrying what insiders have taken to calling the Quarter-Life Gene.

Each member, it is reported, shall upon the occasion of their twenty-fifth birthday receive by electronic post a private subscriber code — granting access to the Gazette's secured archive, there to read the testimonies of their fellow members, and to commit their own reflection to the permanent record.

"We didn't choose the 25 life — the 25 life chose us."

— Anonymous Member, in correspondence with this Gazette

The archive grows with each passing birthday. Reflections, once submitted, are held in perpetuity for all eligible members to peruse. Those whose birthdays are yet to arrive must wait — their entries sealed, their columns blank, their names listed in tomorrow's edition.
